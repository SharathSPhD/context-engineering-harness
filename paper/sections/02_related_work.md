# Background and Related Work

We survey four overlapping literatures and locate Pratyakṣa within each.

## Long-context language models: architectures and limits

The base substrate is the transformer \citep{vaswani2017attention}. Three threads have extended its effective context: (i) *positional generalization* via rotary embeddings \citep{su2021roformer}, ALiBi \citep{press2022alibi}, position interpolation \citep{chen2023pi}, YaRN \citep{peng2023yarn}, and LongRoPE \citep{ding2024longrope}; (ii) *attention-throughput* engineering — FlashAttention 1/2 \citep{dao2022flashattention, dao2023flashattention2}, PagedAttention \citep{kwon2023vllm}, and streaming-attention sinks \citep{xiao2023streamingllm}; and (iii) *alternative state-space architectures* — Mamba \citep{gu2023mamba} and Jamba \citep{lieber2024jamba} — that promise constant-time-per-token recall in principle. Empirically, however, the gap between *advertised* context window and *useful* context window has not closed: Liu et al.~\citeyearpar{liu2023lostmiddle} demonstrated the U-shaped *Lost-in-the-Middle* attention profile; Hsieh et al. \citeyearpar{hsieh2024ruler} measured RULER drop-offs of 30+ pp at the model's stated maximum length; Yen et al. \citeyearpar{yen2024helmet} showed that HELMET accuracy at 32 K is often half that at 4 K; Karpinska et al. \citeyearpar{karpinska2024nocha} found that on NoCha narrative claim verification, even GPT-4-class models score below 30\% joint accuracy on full-novel inputs; Hong et al. \citeyearpar{hong2025contextrot} replicated the failure across every frontier model in 2025. Kazemnejad et al. \citeyearpar{kazemnejad2024impact} attribute much of the residual failure to positional-encoding pathologies that no inference-time fix has yet fully addressed.

**Where Pratyakṣa fits.** We treat the context window as a *typed, witness-tracked, sublatable store* sitting *above* the model's KV cache. We do not modify positional encoding or attention; we modify *what* enters the visible context, *how* it is labelled, and *when* it is superseded. This complements rather than competes with architectural extensions.

## Retrieval-augmented generation, compression, and external memory

RAG \citep{lewis2020rag} and its descendants — HyDE \citep{gao2023hyde}, Self-RAG \citep{asai2024selfrag}, RAPTOR \citep{sarthi2024raptor}, GraphRAG \citep{edge2024graphrag}, REPLUG \citep{shi2023less} — all aim to *put less, but better, text in the prompt*. LLMLingua \citep{jiang2023llmlingua} and LongLLMLingua \citep{jiang2024longllmlingua} compress the prompt token-budget directly. AutoCompressors \citep{chevalier2023autocompressors} learn to fold long prefixes into soft summary tokens. MemGPT/Letta \citep{packer2023memgpt} push the agent toward an "OS-like" tiered memory. Sumers et al. \citeyearpar{sumers2024coala} formalize this lineage as *Cognitive Architectures for Language Agents (CoALA)*. Voyager \citep{wang2024voyager} demonstrates a long-running embodied agent built on a curriculum-style external skill library.

**Where Pratyakṣa fits.** Two differences. (i) Pratyakṣa's store carries *typed limitor conditions* (Nyāya *avacchedaka*) — "X holds *as Y* *under conditions Z*" — rather than free text. This makes conflicts explicit and resolvable. (ii) Conflict resolution is *bādha* (sublation), not deletion: superseded items remain queryable for audit, but their *qualifier* is rewritten so they no longer contribute to the live answer. We replace the prevalent vector-similarity-and-reranking pipeline with a small **Bayesian Beta-Bernoulli** aggregator (Section 5.3) that consumes per-source precision and yields a calibrated posterior, evaluated by Brier score and ECE \citep{naeini2015ece, brier1950score, guo2017calibration}.

## Agents, multi-agent coordination, and agentic coding

Cognition AI's *Don't Build Multi-Agents* \citep{yan2025dontbuild} crystallized the tension: parallel sub-agents look attractive but their context overlap is fragile, decisions made on partial state are routinely wrong, and the surface area for hallucination explodes. Anthropic's *Effective Context Engineering* \citep{anthropic2025contexteng} reaches the same conclusion from the host-platform side: a single agent with disciplined context tends to outperform a federation. Tracks like ReAct \citep{yao2023react} and Reflexion \citep{shinn2023reflexion} extend single-agent reasoning with explicit tool-use and self-critique; DSPy \citep{khattab2023dspy} compiles such pipelines.

In the *coding agent* sub-genre specifically, SWE-bench \citep{jimenez2024swebench} and SWE-bench Verified \citep{openai2024sweverified} dominate the evaluation surface; OpenHands \citep{wang2024openhands}, Claude Code \citep{anthropic2025claudecode}, Aider \citep{aider2024}, Devin \citep{cognition2024devin}, MetaGPT \citep{hong2024metagpt}, ChatDev \citep{chen2024chatdev}, and AgentBench \citep{liu2023agentbench} represent the contending agentic frameworks. The dominant failure mode reported across all of them is *agent anchoring on stale or misleading evidence in its working set* — the *Lost-in-the-Middle* and RULER-style anchoring biases of \citet{liu2023lostmiddle} and \citet{hsieh2024ruler} — which is exactly the situation our P6-C study reproduces.

**Where Pratyakṣa fits.** We are deliberately single-agent-by-default. Buddhi and Manas are internal *gates* on one agent's reasoning trace — Manas filters evidence using avacchedaka conditions, Buddhi judges against accepted *pramāṇas* — not separate networked agents. Sākṣī is a *witness object* providing a stable reference frame for cross-turn provenance, not a supervisor. The plugin is therefore on the right side of Cognition's principle: *one agent, full history, typed memory*.

## Hallucination, calibration, and grounding

The two foundational hallucination surveys \citep{ji2023hallusurvey, huang2024hallucinationsurvey} agree that the field has *no* widely-adopted typology. HaluEval \citep{li2023halueval} and TruthfulQA \citep{lin2022truthfulqa} test specific failure surfaces (hallucinated QA spans, misconception traps); Anthropic's FACTS-Grounding \citep{anthropic2024facts} measures faithfulness to a provided source. Calibration metrics — Brier score \citep{brier1950score}, ECE \citep{naeini2015ece, guo2017calibration}, "Just Ask for Calibration" \citep{tian2023justask}, and the broader "models mostly know what they know" framing \citep{kadavath2022know} — try to assess *whether the model knows it is uncertain*, but few systems use those signals at runtime.

**Where Pratyakṣa fits.** We import a *six-class* error taxonomy from classical Indian philosophy — *anyathākhyāti* (apprehension of the object as something else, e.g. wrong API name), *ātmakhyāti* (projection of the agent's own state onto the world, e.g. inventing helper functions the framework never exposed), *anirvacanīyakhyāti* (apprehension of an indeterminate object, e.g. hedged hallucinations that "feel right"), *asatkhyāti* (apprehension of the non-existent, e.g. quoting a CVE that never existed), *viparītakhyāti* (apprehension of the contrary, e.g. inverting the boolean of a flag), and *akhyāti* (failure of correct apprehension by mere conflation, e.g. mixing up two adjacent functions) \citep{ram2007error, matilal1986perception, bilimoria2018epistemology}. We attach the shipped `classify_khyativada` tool (heuristic + guardrails; JSON-structured) to flagged answers, and additionally study a few-shot Anthropic JSON variant in the experiments harness (not wired into the plugin). Inter-annotator agreement is Cohen's κ \citep{cohen1960kappa, landis1977kappa} = 0.736 on n = 3,000 examples (§8.6). To our knowledge no prior LLM hallucination work attaches a single, philosophically pre-committed 6-class taxonomy with documented IAA at this scale.

## Cognitive science antecedents we are *not* claiming as ours

Many of Pratyakṣa's mechanisms have parallels in cognitive science. Working-memory models \citep{baddeley1974wm, baddeley2000episodic, cowan2001focus, miller1956magical} predicted finite, qualifier-tagged stores half a century ago. Prefrontal cortex acts as a precision-weighted attention controller \citep{miller2001integrative, corbetta2002dorsalventral, desimone1995biased, reynolds2009gain}, with attractor-network dynamics in delay-period activity \citep{compte2000attractor, funahashi1989delay} and "activity-silent" reactivation in beta/gamma bursts \citep{stokes2015activitysilent, lundqvist2016gamma}. The complementary-learning-systems theory \citep{mcclelland1995cls, kumaran2016cls} prescribes exactly the dual short-term/long-term store our adaptive forgetting respects. Predictive-coding and free-energy accounts \citep{rao1999predictive, friston2010fep, feldman2010precision} provide the precision-weighting we approximate with surprise-driven event boundaries. Schema theory \citep{bartlett1932remembering, ghosh2014schema, vankesteren2012slimm} and event-segmentation \citep{zacks2007event, baldassano2017nested} predict the EventBoundaryCompactor's behaviour. Sleep-mediated replay \citep{wilson1994replay, ambrose2016reward, diekelmann2010sleep, buzsaki2015ripples} and complementary-learning-systems-style consolidation \citep{richards2017persistence} predict our adaptive-forgetting policy.

We are *informed* by these literatures, but we make no claim to model the brain. Our claim is the inverse: *classical Indian epistemology* — specifically the **Nyāya–Vaiśeṣika**, **Advaita Vedānta**, **Pūrva Mīmāṃsā**, and **Sāṃkhya** schools — *gives us a vocabulary* and a typed object model that **happens to be operationalisable** in LLM context engineering, and *happens to be coherent* with what cognitive neuroscience independently discovered. The cross-cultural agreement is part of why we believe the constructs are real. Classical Indian theorists — Ākṣapāda Gautama \citep{aksapada200nyayasutra}, Praśastapāda \citep{prasastapada6padarthadharma}, Śaṅkara \citep{sankaraupadesha}, Dharmarāja \citep{dharmaraja17vedantaparibhasa}, Gaṅgeśa \citep{gangesa14tattvacintamani} — and modern interpreters — Matilal \citep{matilal1986perception, matilal1968navyanyaya}, Ganeri \citep{ganeri2001philosophy, ganeri2017concealed}, Mohanty \citep{mohanty1992reason}, Phillips \citep{phillips2012epistemology, phillips1997classical}, Ram-Prasad \citep{ramprasad2013advaita, ram2007error}, Dasti \citep{dasti2008jayanta} — supply the technical apparatus we draw on directly.

## Cognitive architectures and dual-process accounts

Classical AI cognitive architectures — SOAR \citep{laird1987soar}, ACT-R \citep{anderson1996actr}, Newell's *Unified Theories of Cognition* \citep{newell1990unified} — formalised the working-memory/long-term-memory split and procedural production rules that motivated our Buddhi/Manas split. Dual-process theories of human cognition \citep{evans2003duality, kahneman2011thinking, sloman1996two, stanovich2000individual, epstein1994integration} and Halford et al.'s relational complexity theory \citep{halford1998processing} furnish the empirical backdrop for the two-stage gate.

## What we do *not* contribute

We do *not* contribute: a new model architecture; a new pre-training regime; a new positional encoding; a new attention kernel; a new vector-database backend. We *contribute*: a typed, philosophically-named, plugin-deliverable **discipline** for the context window of an existing LLM agent, plus the empirical case that this discipline pays off at p < 10⁻¹⁹.
