\section{12 · Conclusions and Future Work}\label{conclusions-and-future-work}

\subsection{12.1 What we did}\label{what-we-did}

We presented \textbf{Pratyakṣa}, a context-engineering harness for LLM coding agents, packaged as a Cursor- and Claude-Code-compatible plugin (15 MCP tools, 3 skills, 3 agents, 4 commands, 3 lifecycle hooks, MIT-licensed, installable in 30 seconds). The harness operationalizes seven constructs from classical Indian epistemology --- \emph{pratyakṣa, avacchedaka, bādha, buddhi/manas, sākṣī, khyātivāda, saṃskāra/vāsanā} --- into runtime mechanisms on the agent's working context. We validated across three orthogonal evidence layers: seven preregistered hypotheses on five public benchmarks plus SWE-bench Verified (L1, paired multi-seed multi-model), a deterministic live case study on three real GitHub issues (L2), and a 720-pair head-to-head A/B on 120 SWE-bench Verified instances under a fixed token budget (L3). Combining the 10 quantitative studies via weighted Stouffer-Z gives a two-sided p of \textbf{7.94 × 10⁻²⁰} in the harness's favour, with mean per-study delta \textbf{+0.476} and Cohen's d ≈ \textbf{1.0} on the most ecologically valid study. The Khyātivāda 6-class hallucination annotator achieves Cohen's κ = \textbf{0.736} (``substantial'') on n = 3,000 examples.

\subsection{12.2 What we claim}\label{what-we-claim}

We claim the \emph{modest} version of three things:

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\item
  \textbf{Vedic epistemology supplies usable type signatures for context engineering.} Avacchedaka, bādha, buddhi/manas, sākṣī, and khyātivāda are not metaphors; they admit precise runtime operationalizations on an LLM context window, and those operationalizations measurably improve downstream agent behaviour.
\item
  \textbf{A drop-in plugin is the right delivery vehicle.} No model fine-tune, no client patch, no infrastructure dependency heavier than \texttt{tiktoken} and \texttt{pydantic}. The harness installs in 30 seconds onto Cursor, Claude Code (CLI / VS Code), or Claude Desktop and is hot-swappable across all four hosts because the only inter-process surface is MCP.
\item
  \textbf{The harness improves performance under fixed token budgets}, \emph{not} by buying more context but by using the same budget more disciplinedly. This is the orthogonal axis to the architectural-extensions literature \citep{chen2023pi, peng2023yarn, ding2024longrope, gu2023mamba, lieber2024jamba} and the compression-extensions literature \citep{jiang2023llmlingua, jiang2024longllmlingua, chevalier2023autocompressors}.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{12.3 What we do \emph{not} claim}{12.3 What we do not claim}}\label{what-we-do-not-claim}

We do \emph{not} claim a new model architecture, a new pre-training regime, a new positional encoding, or a new attention kernel. We do \emph{not} claim that the historical Sanskrit authors had LLMs in mind or that our operationalizations are textually faithful in a way a Sanskrit philologist would require. We do \emph{not} claim our synthetic-fallback adapters reproduce the absolute numbers of published RULER / HELMET runs --- only that the \emph{paired delta in the harness's favour} is a faithful estimate. We do \emph{not} claim that the L3 PatchSimulator's gains will fully transfer to a real LLM coder; Section 11 names this as the cleanest near-term gap.

\subsection{12.4 Future work}\label{future-work}

Eight follow-ups are well-defined enough to scope today:

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\item
  \textbf{Cross-family LLM sweep.} Run the L1+L3 pipeline against GPT-4o-class, Qwen-3-72B, Llama-3.x. The harness should not change; the deltas may.
\item
  \textbf{Real-LLM-coder P6-C.} Replace the deterministic PatchSimulator with a real Claude/GPT/Qwen coder and remeasure target-path-hit-rate, full pass-rate, and SWE-bench Verified pass@1.
\item
  \textbf{Human-vs-human Khyātivāda IAA at n ≥ 1,000.} Promote the κ = 0.736 from automated-vs-automated to human-validated, with at least three annotators.
\item
  \textbf{Live-deployment telemetry.} Ship the plugin to a small pilot group of Cursor / Claude Code users and measure (consensually) the rate of \texttt{sublate\_with\_evidence} events, the post-Buddhi \texttt{khyati\_class} distribution, and per-session token-budget gauge curves.
\item
  \textbf{Non-coding agentic surfaces.} Apply the harness to a research-assistant or customer-service agent and measure the per-domain analogue of ``target-path-hit-rate''.
\item
  \textbf{Philological refinement.} Engage Sanskritists to refine the avacchedaka, bādha, and khyātivāda operationalizations. Some commitments --- e.g.~our 4-tuple representation of avacchedaka --- likely under-respect the Navya-Nyāya tradition's richer relational logic \citep{matilal1968navyanyaya, ingalls1951materials, gangesa14tattvacintamani}.
\item
  \textbf{Cognitive-neuroscience cross-checks.} The complementary-learning-systems prediction \citep{mcclelland1995cls, kumaran2016cls} is testable: witness-protected items should show consolidation curves that differ from non-protected items. We have not measured this.
\item
  \textbf{A shared runtime contract for context-engineering plugins.} Following Cognition's \emph{Don't Build Multi-Agents} \citep{yan2025dontbuild} and Anthropic's context-engineering position \citep{anthropic2025contexteng}, a shared MCP-side schema for typed limitor + sublation + witness-tracking would let any host platform interoperate. We propose \texttt{pratyaksha-context-eng-harness} as a candidate reference implementation and welcome co-design.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{12.5 A closing methodological note}\label{a-closing-methodological-note}

The work began with TRIZ (Section 3) --- a single contradiction, a contradiction-matrix lookup, a chosen inventive principle (\#10 Preliminary Action), and the recognition that the principle required a vocabulary the modern LLM literature did not have. That vocabulary was sitting, fully developed, in classical Indian epistemology. The harness is the operationalization of that recognition. We retain the audit trail --- \texttt{docs/triz-origin-session.md}, \texttt{docs/v0\_retrospective.md}, the \texttt{cost\_ledger.db}, the \texttt{witness\_log.jsonl} --- because the kind of provenance we ask the \emph{agent} to maintain is the kind of provenance we maintained for ourselves while building it. \emph{Pratyakṣa is its own first user.}

The shipped plugin, the full reproducibility manifest, the 246-entry bibliography, the 13 figures, and the 7 tables are released at \texttt{github.com/SharathSPhD/pratyaksha-context-eng-harness} under MIT licence. We hope it is useful.
